The market has changed. Your sale needs to change with it.

If you're trying to sell a house in Atlanta, Tulsa, Tampa Bay, or Seattle right now, you already know: this isn't the market of 2021. Homes are sitting on the market for months — even good homes in good neighborhoods. Buyers are picky. Properties in as-is condition struggle to find buyers at all. Sellers are accepting prices they don't want, or watching their listings rot for 6–12+ months.

Frequently asked

Common questions from sellers

How do you calculate your cash offer?
We pull comparable sales ("comps") from your neighborhood over the last 90 days, estimate the after-repair value, subtract the renovation costs needed to bring the house to retail condition, and subtract our fair profit margin. We'll walk you through the math line-by-line on the call — no black box.
Is there any obligation to accept your offer?
Zero. We present an offer, you review it, and if it doesn't work for you, you walk away with no strings. We'd rather lose a deal than pressure anyone.
What if my house needs a lot of repairs?
That's exactly what we specialize in. Fire damage, foundation issues, hoarder conditions, mold — we've bought them all. We handle the repairs after closing. You don't have to lift a finger.
Will I get less than market value?
You'll typically get less than the full retail market value — but when you factor in the 6% agent commission you won't pay, the $5–15k in repairs an inspector would demand, 2-3 months of mortgage and utility payments while listed, and closing costs we cover, the net in your pocket is often within 2–5% of a retail sale, and you close in days instead of months.
Can you help with probate or inherited property?
Yes — we work with executors, heirs, and probate attorneys regularly. We can close as soon as probate is final, and in many states we can start the paperwork while it's still pending. Share your situation and we'll walk you through what's possible.
Who covers closing costs?
We do. All of them. Title, transfer tax, recording fees — every standard closing cost. Your net at closing is the offer price, full stop.

⚠ Tulsa market reality check

Tulsa's market has shifted in favor of buyers.

Tulsa, Broken Arrow, Jenks, Bixby — across Green Country, homes are sitting on the market far longer than they did in 2021–2022. Buyers are picky. Properties that need work are particularly hard to move. Sellers who can't afford major repairs are getting squeezed, and traditional listings take 4–6+ months with price reductions being the norm.

For Tulsa sellers specifically, there's another wrinkle: many owners bought between 2020–2022 at higher prices with low-rate mortgages. Selling outright triggers mortgage payoff and lost equity. Subject-to deals solve that problem. Seller financing often nets more than cash. And our investor network — hedge funds, family offices, professional investors — frequently bids more aggressively than local cash buyers.

The "cash offer only" model that dominates Tulsa's market (House Hunters US, Easy Home Offers, and similar) doesn't serve sellers whose situations don't fit that box. We offer five structures for exactly that reason.
